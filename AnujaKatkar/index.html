History

India has a rich maritime history dating back to ancient times, when people used boats for trade and travel. Early civilizations like the Indus Valley traded with regions such as Mesopotamia through sea routes. Indian sailors were skilled in navigation and shipbuilding. This strong maritime tradition helped India connect with many parts of the world.

Boat Types

1. Canoe : A canoe is a small, narrow boat usually moved using paddles. It was commonly used in ancient times for fishing and travelling on rivers.

2. Rowboat : A rowboat is a small boat powered by oars. It is simple to use and mainly used in lakes and calm waters.

3. Sailboat : A sailboat uses wind power to move with the help of sails. Ancient Indian sailors used sailboats for long sea journeys and trade.

4. Motorboat : A motorboat runs with the help of an engine. It is faster and used today for transport, rescue, and tourism.

5. Ship : A ship is a large water vessel used for carrying goods and people across oceans. It plays an important role in international trade.

6. Fishing Boat : Fishing boats are specially designed for catching fish. They can be small or large depending on the fishing activity.

7. Cargo Ship : Cargo ships are used to transport heavy goods like containers, oil, and raw materials across countries.

Navigation

Navigation helps sailors travel safely from one place to another across oceans and rivers. In ancient times, Indian mariners used the sun, stars, and natural signs like waves and winds. Over time, tools like the compass and maps improved navigation, making sea travel easier and more reliable..
