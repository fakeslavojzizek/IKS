Ancient Games

Kalaripayattu

Origin: Kerala, India

Type: Martial Art

Description: One of the oldest martial arts in the world from Kerala, India. It involves kicks, punches, weapons, body control, advanced weapon training such as swords, spears, flexible weapons, and focuses on Marma (pressure point) techniques and traditional healing methods. It is an important cultural heritage of Kerala.

Importance: Improves strength, flexibility, discipline, and self-defense skills while preserving traditional Indian heritage.

Mallakhamb

Origin: Maharashtra, India

Type: Gymnastics

Description: Traditional Indian sport from Maharashtra where athletes perform yoga and acrobatic movements on a wooden pole or rope. It includes Pole Mallakhamb and Rope Mallakhamb and was originally used to train wrestlers by improving agility, flexibility, grip strength, and body balance. It is now practiced in national level competitions.

Importance: Develops full-body strength, balance, flexibility, focus, and is used for training wrestlers and gymnasts.

Chaturanga

Origin: Ancient India

Type: Board Game

Description: Ancient Indian strategy game played on an 8x8 board, considered the origin of modern chess. It represented the four divisions of the ancient army—infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots—and helped players develop strategic thinking, planning, and decision-making skills.

Importance: Improves logical thinking, concentration, planning ability, and strategic decision-making skills.

Pachisi

Origin: India

Type: Board Game

Description: Traditional Indian cross-shaped board game played using cowrie shells, considered the ancient form of Ludo. It was popular in royal courts such as Emperor Akbar’s court and combines both luck and strategy as players move tokens based on dice-like shell throws.

Importance: Enhances patience, counting skills, strategic thinking, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Chariot Racing

Origin: Ancient Rome & Greece

Type: Racing Sport

Description: Ancient high-speed racing sport where horses pulled chariots in large arenas such as Circus Maximus in Rome. It was extremely dangerous and exciting, often involving crashes, and was one of the most popular entertainment forms in ancient civilizations.

Importance: Builds courage, excitement, and represents ancient sports culture, teamwork, and speed skills.
