16 Vedic Mathematics Sutras

1. Ekadhikena Purvena

Means "By one more than the previous one". Commonly used for squaring numbers ending in 5.

Example: 25² = 625

2. Nikhilam Navatashcaramam Dashatah

Means "All from 9 and the last from 10". Used for multiplication near powers of 10.

Example: 98 × 97 = 9506

3. Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam

Means "Vertically and Crosswise". Fast multiplication method for all numbers.

Example: 23 × 14 = 322

4. Paravartya Yojayet

Means "Transpose and Adjust". Used in division and algebraic equations.

Example: Solve 2x+3=11 → x=4

5. Shunyam Saamyasamuccaye

When sums are equal, that sum is zero. Helpful in solving equations.

Example: x+5 = 5+x

6. Anurupyena

Means "Proportionately". Used when numbers are in proportion.

Example: 2/4 = 4/8

7. Sankalana-Vyavakalanabhyam

Means "By Addition and Subtraction". Used for simultaneous equations.

Example: x+y=10, x−y=2

8. Puranapuranabhyam

Means "By Completion or Non-Completion". Used in complex calculations.

Example: Completing square method

9. Chalana-Kalanabhyam

Means "Differences and Similarities". Used in calculus-like operations.

Example: Difference of squares

10. Yaavadunam

Means "Whatever the Deficiency". Used for numbers below base.

Example: 96² = 9216

11. Vyashtisamanstih

Means "Part and Whole". Used in factorization and algebra.

Example: 12 = 10 + 2

12. Shesanyankena Charamena

Means "Remainders by Last Digit". Used in division shortcuts.

Example: Divisibility tricks

13. Sopantyadvayamantyam

Means "Ultimate and Twice the Penultimate". Used in equations.

Example: Polynomial solving shortcuts

14. Ekanyunena Purvena

Means "By One Less than Previous One". Used for multiplication by 9s.

Example: 9 × 8 = 72

15. Gunitasamuccayah

Product of sum equals sum of product. Used in algebra verification.

Example: (a+b)(c+d)

16. Gunakasamuccayah

Factors of sum equal sum of factors. Used in factorization.

Example: Factoring expressions
