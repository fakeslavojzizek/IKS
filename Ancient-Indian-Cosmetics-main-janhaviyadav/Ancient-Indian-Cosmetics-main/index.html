Ancient Indian Cosmetics

Ancient Indian cosmetics were a natural and holistic system of beauty based on Ayurveda, combining health, science, and spirituality. They were made using herbal ingredients like turmeric, sandalwood, oils, and flowers, prepared through simple methods such as grinding and mixing. These cosmetics were not only used to enhance appearance but also had medicinal benefits like improving skin health, protecting the body, and promoting relaxation. Practices like ubtan, kajal, and oiling were part of daily life. Ancient texts such as the Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita highlight how beauty was closely connected with health and spiritual well-being.
