Ancient Roots of a Modern Science

Long before the invention of the microscope, ancient Indian scholars like Sushruta and Charaka possessed profound a knowledge of invisible, disease-causing entities. They referred to them using terms such as 'Krimi' and 'Rakshasa' (pathogens), detailing their presence in blood, water, and air.

The ancient texts of Ayurveda (the science of life) emphasized sterilization, hygiene, and the use of potent antimicrobial herbs like Neem, Turmeric, and Tulsi. Advanced civilization practices found in the Indus Valley further indicate an innate understanding of environmental sanitation and water microbiology.

Join us on a journey to rediscover how a civilization harmonized with the unseen world entirely through observation and natural sciences.
