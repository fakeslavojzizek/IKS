Historical Foreign Accounts

Observations of Travelers

Beyond native texts, the microbiological and hygiene standards of Ancient India were rigorously corroborated by awe-struck foreign chroniclers. When the Greek ethnographer Megasthenes visited the Mauryan Empire (c. 300 BCE), he extensively documented the unmatched health and longevity of the Indians, attributing it to their strict dietary hygiene and water processing.

Centuries later, Chinese Buddhist monks like Xuanzang and Faxian traveled through India and noted the compulsive habits of the populace—frequent bathing routines, meticulously brushing teeth with antimicrobial neem twigs, and strict rules isolating the sick to prevent the miasmatic spread of disease.
