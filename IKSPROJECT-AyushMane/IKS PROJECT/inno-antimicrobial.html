Use of Antimicrobial Substances

Harnessing Natural Disinfectants

The innovation here lies not just in using herbs, but in systematically categorizing botanicals and metals explicitly for their antimicrobial properties. The Charaka Samhita acts as a repository of knowledge on hundreds of antimicrobial plants.

Neem (Azadirachta indica), turmeric (Curcuma longa), and clove were ground in stone mortars and utilized constantly to scrub wounds, purify air, and sterilize environments. Scientists now know that curcumin (in turmeric) rapidly destroys bacterial cell membranes.
